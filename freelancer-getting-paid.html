Getting paid as a freelancer, without losing 5% to your bank

Three ways your clients can pay you, what each one costs, and when the money actually shows up.

💸

The three ways clients pay freelancers

  • Marketplace payout — clients hire you on Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal, or similar. The platform pays you.
  • Direct invoice — you send the client bank details and they pay you directly.
  • Payment request — you send a payment link and the client pays by card or local bank transfer.
A typical $5,000 international wire to a local bank can lose $80–$150 to intermediary deductions and FX margin before you ever see it.

Why your local bank is rarely the best receiving option

When a US client wires $5,000 to a freelancer's local bank account abroad, the wire often arrives smaller than expected — intermediary banks deduct fees, and the receiving bank converts to local currency at a poor rate. A USD receiving account lets the client pay via ACH (often free for them) and lets you choose when to convert.

Withdrawing to your local bank

From your GlobalPay balance, you can withdraw to your local bank account in your home currency. Withdrawals typically arrive in 1 to 3 business days, and the conversion rate applied is the rate displayed at the time you initiate the withdrawal.
