USD receiving accounts for non-US businesses

A US routing number and account number you can put on an invoice — without opening a US bank account.

🏦

What it is

A USD receiving account gives non-US businesses and freelancers a set of US bank account details: a 9-digit ABA routing number and an account number. From the payer's side, sending you USD looks identical to paying a domestic US vendor — which means your US clients can pay you over ACH instead of an expensive international wire.

What you can receive

  • ACH credits from US customers and payroll systems
  • Domestic US wires
  • Payouts from US marketplaces (Amazon US, Walmart, Etsy, Upwork, Fiverr, and others)
A USD receiving account is not a US bank account — it's a virtual receiving facility through GlobalPay's US banking partners. Funds settle into your GlobalPay balance.

Who it's for

Freelancers and agencies invoicing US clients, e-commerce sellers receiving US marketplace payouts, and SaaS companies billing US customers without forcing them to wire internationally.
