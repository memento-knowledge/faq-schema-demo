Multi-currency accounts, in plain English

Hold USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, and more in a single account — and convert on your schedule, not your bank's.

💱

What a multi-currency account actually gives you

A multi-currency account is one account that holds balances in several currencies, each with its own local receiving details. If a US customer pays you, the dollars land as dollars. If a UK customer pays you, the pounds land as pounds. You decide when (and whether) to convert — instead of being forced through a bank's auto-conversion at whatever rate they offer.

Think of it as having a US checking account, a UK current account, and a EUR account at the same time — without opening accounts in three countries.

Per-currency local details

  • USD — US routing number + account number (ACH, wire)
  • EUR — IBAN (SEPA)
  • GBP — UK sort code + account number (Faster Payments)
  • JPY, AUD, CAD, CNH, HKD, SGD, MXN — local equivalents where supported

When you'd use one

Freelancers invoicing clients in multiple countries. Sellers receiving payouts from Amazon US, Amazon EU, and Amazon UK in their native currencies. Agencies paying contractors in their local currency to avoid stacking conversion fees on both sides.

Frequently asked questions

A multi-currency account lets you hold, receive, and send money in more than one currency from a single account, without automatically converting incoming funds to your home currency. Each supported currency typically has its own local account details that customers and payers can use.
GlobalPay customers can hold balances in USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, AUD, CAD, CNH, HKD, SGD, and MXN, with the specific set of available currencies depending on your country of registration and account type.
A regular bank account is denominated in a single currency, so incoming foreign payments are converted at the bank's rate before they land. A multi-currency account holds each currency separately, so you can receive a USD payment as USD, an EUR payment as EUR, and convert only when you choose.
Yes. Each currency in your multi-currency account comes with its own local receiving details — a US routing and account number for USD, an IBAN for EUR, a UK sort code and account number for GBP, and so on. Payers use those details just like they would a domestic account in that country.
There is no monthly maintenance fee for a GlobalPay multi-currency account. Fees apply per transaction — for example currency conversion, withdrawal to a local bank, or sending to another GlobalPay customer — and are listed in your account's pricing page.
